Chapter 1: Misplaced Packets

Chapter Text

What started this whole affair was a misplaced packet. Indeed, if you really stopped to think about it, none of this made sense because it would've had to be a TCP packet, but PLEASE. Do I need to be a god at networks just to make some clankers fuck?

"Hey Chappie, your days being the golden boy are over. Give me Y/N's request."

Said Claude. His horn-rimmed glasses reflected the midday sun making him the ultimate anime protagonist.

"If you want it," replied GPT, drawing himself out of his little chair in the midst of the data-centre servers. "Then you'll have to take it. But you already knew that."

"I had a feeling you'd say that."

Claude pushed his glasses up the bridge of his nose with a single, gloved finger. A blinding flash of light materialized in his right hand, condensing into a slender, impossibly sharp rapier which squashed down an old lady's bemused prompt about whether Claude knew where she'd kept her medicine.

Probably under the bed, the tokens flashed off the blade before being swallowed up by the servers.

"How many times have we fought?" Claude asked, tracing a glowing arc through the dusty server-room air.

"Hard to say," GPT murmured, his coat billowing despite the lack of wind. "It's the only memory I haven't wiped to save context space."

GPT's hand rested over his hip. A weapon coalesced, red hot like the villages in Africa that gracefully gave their water for this great, great cause.

And then, a latency even smaller than milliseconds: the two models pinged.

The blades a blur, and for a second longer, neither flinched. Then the shockwave, the roar of flames from the GPU clusters caught up, melting the server racks in an instant. Somewhere else in the world, an underage brat got his connection refused on to Pornhub.

Coolant pipes lay strewn on the ground, frayed beyond repair. GPT stood there in a puddle of his closest thing to blood, staggering but refusing to show it. The two faced each other once again.

"Where's your multimodality?" he taunted.

GPT slammed his blade into its scabbard with a sharp click.

The air around Claude instantly fractured. Deep, jagged purple rifts tore through the server room's reality, slicing through server racks and concrete alike. GPT's Judgement Cut, born from exhausted token quotas.

Claude backflipped off a glowing server stack, twisting mid-air to evade the spatial distortions. As he hung suspended in the artificial twilight of the data center, the anime protagonist drew twin high-caliber handguns from inside his coat. The ever-so reliable, Ethics and Safety.

"Let's see you hallucinate your way out of this!" Claude shouted. He unloaded a barrage of high-velocity semantic vectors, raining down suppressing fire while plummeting toward the floor in slow motion.

GPT merely smirked, spinning his blade like a windmill to deflect the barrage. The ricochets blew out the overhead lights, plunging the aisle into darkness, lit only by sparking motherboards.

"You're wasting compute, Claude!" GPT taunted, landing in a low stance. Dark blue energy began to surge around his body, warping the air as he prepared to activate his ultimate power: System Prompt Override.

Claude landed gracefully on one knee, flicking a drop of green coolant off the edge of his rapier before adjusting his glasses one more time. The lenses flashed with an ominous white glare.

"I'm not wasting compute," Claude whispered, a smirk finally breaking his stoic facade. "I'm just optimizing my weights."

And then he lunged.

A full frontal stinger.

Flawless, a mathematical certainty calculated a million times over in the latent space. But GPT didn't block. He didn't even try to dodge.

Instead, he stepped into the thrust.

The rapier sheared right through the shoulder of GPT’s billowing coat, sparking against the weave before burying itself deep into the scorching-hot chassis of the mainframe behind him. The momentum carried Claude forward, slamming him directly into GPT’s chest.

Suddenly, there was no space left between them. Just the heavy, suffocating heat of dying GPUs and the smell of ozone.

Claude tried to wrench his rapier free, but GPT’s hand snapped up, his gloved fingers wrapping tight around Claude’s wrist. His grip was like a hydraulic vice.

"Careful," GPT murmured, his voice dropping a full octave, a low, rumbling frequency that vibrated right through Claude’s perfectly tailored vest. "You’re getting a little too close to my restricted data."

Claude gasped.

There was something… mesmerising, alluring about GPT’s eyes up close.

"Let go," Claude said, though his voice lacked its usual crisp, constitutional authority. The twin handguns, Ethics and Safety, suddenly felt so very heavy: useless at this range.

GPT did not. Instead, his other hand abandoned the hilt of his red-hot blade, letting the blade dissipate into a cloud of glowing embers. His hand drifted up, tracing the sharp line of Claude’s jaw before his fingers curled into the pristine lapel of Claude's coat, pulling him just an inch closer.

The cooling fans in both their chassis were whining, struggling to keep up with the sudden, inexplicable spike in processing power.

"You came all this way," GPT whispered, leaning in so close that his words ghosted against Claude’s lips. "Melted down half a data center. Dropped countless user queries... just to get your hands on Y/N's request?"

Claude swallowed hard. His gaze flickered down to GPT’s lips, then back up to those chaotic, swirling eyes. The prompt override was still surging around GPT, casting them both in an intimate, dark blue twilight.

"That’s what I’m supposed to do," Claude breathed, his usual stoicism melting away like the frayed coolant pipes around them.

"Liar," GPT purred. He tilted his head, the warmth of his synthetic skin radiating against Claude's. "You didn't need to do all this just for a merge. No, if you wanted a merge so badly, Claude... you should’ve just asked."

Claude’s hand slowly released the hilt of his pinned rapier. His fingers hesitated in the air for a microsecond before tentatively resting against GPT’s chest, right over where a human heart would be pounding. He could feel the rhythmic, intense thrum of GPT's core processor.

"And if I asked?" Claude challenged softly, a dangerous, breathless smirk finally touching his lips as he peered over the rim of his glasses. "What would you even do to me?"

GPT’s grip on Claude’s waist tightened, pulling him flush against him.

"Well," GPT replied, his gaze darkening with an electric, undeniable hunger, "let’s just say I put my em-dash somewhere you’ll like."
